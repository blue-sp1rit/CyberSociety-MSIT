What do we stand for?

We strive to make cybersecurity education accessible and engaging for everyone, as we believe it is crucial for creating a safer and more secure digital environment. We encourage innovation and experimentation in cybersecurity education and recognize the importance of pushing boundaries and staying up to date. We empower individuals with the tools they need to stay ahead in cybersecurity and learn alongside their peers.

Who Are We?

We are a group of students dedicated to learning and sharing knowledge in the field of cybersecurity. Our community aims to provide a platform for collaboration, hands-on practice, and discussions on various areas within cybersecurity. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced professional, our goal is to create a space where everyone can develop their skills and contribute to building a strong, supportive community.

Beginner

1. PicoCTF: Great for students and beginners

2. TryHackMe: Interactive labs for various skill levels

3. RootMe: Practice with real vulnerabilities

4. OverTheWire: Classic wargames to build your foundational skills

5. Vulnhub: Download vulnerable machines for offline practice

6. Hack This Site: Learn WebApp security and Exploitation

Intermediate

1. Hack The Box: Test your skills on active challenges

2. Google CTF: Participate in Google's annual CTF

3. CMD Challenge: Test your bash and shell command skills

4. PentesterLab: Learn pentesting with hands-on exercises

Specialized

1. Pwn College: Focus on binary exploitation and pwning

2. PortSwigger Web Security Academy: Learn about web vulnerabilities

3. CryptoHack: Sharpen your cryptography skills

4. Guyinatuxedo GitHub: Learn Reverse engineering skills

Advanced

1. Attack-Defense: Realistic attack-defense scenarios

2. Hacker101 CTF: CTF challenges designed by HackerOne

3. Komodo Security CTF: Application security-focused CTF
